Privacy
Policy

ChooTrack is a UK rail departures and tracking app. It has no accounts, no analytics, no advertising, and no third-party trackers. This page explains, in plain English, exactly what data the app handles and where it goes.

No account No analytics SDK No ad SDK No tracking No data sold

The short version

You never sign up, never log in, and are never identified. ChooTrack contains zero third-party dependencies: no analytics library, no advertising library, no crash-reporting or attribution SDK. Your pinned stations, recent searches and settings live on your device. The only things ChooTrack's own backend ever stores are the details of a journey you have explicitly chosen to track, so it can push live updates to your Lock Screen, and those are deleted when you stop tracking.

1. Who we are

ChooTrack is an independent iOS app published by Ted Roubour. It is not affiliated with National Rail, Network Rail, or any train operating company. For anything in this policy you can reach us at the address in section 11.

2. No accounts, no identifiers

  • There is no sign-up, no login, and no user account of any kind.
  • ChooTrack does not ask for your name, email address, phone number, or date of birth.
  • ChooTrack does not create an advertising identifier or a persistent user profile.
  • ChooTrack does not use App Tracking Transparency, because it does not track you across other apps or websites.

3. Location

Location is used for exactly one thing: the “Guess my train” feature, which works out which live service you are most likely sitting on.

  • Location is requested only when you tap “Guess my train”. Nothing happens in the background, and the app does not use Always-On or background location.
  • The app makes a single, one-shot When-In-Use location request. If you decline the permission, the rest of the app works normally; only that one feature is unavailable.
  • The resulting coordinate is sent to ChooTrack's backend for the sole purpose of matching you to nearby live train services.
  • The coordinate is not stored: not saved on your device, not written to a database, and not written to the server's logs. It exists only for the duration of that one request.
  • Your location is never shared with advertisers, data brokers, or any other third party.

The same one-shot approach is used if you ask the app to list stations near you. You can revoke location access at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services → ChooTrack.

4. What stays on your device

The following are stored locally on your iPhone and are not transmitted to us:

  • Pinned / favourite stations.
  • Recent searches and recently-viewed services.
  • Your home station.
  • App settings and theme choice.

This data is also written to the app's shared App Group container so that the home-screen widget and Live Activity can read it. That container is a private area on your own device, readable only by ChooTrack and its own extensions.

None of it leaves your device except in the obvious, unavoidable sense that looking at a departure board sends that station's code to a rail data service so it can return the board. Deleting the app removes all of it.

5. Tracked journeys and push notifications

When you choose to track a train, ChooTrack's backend needs to know which train to watch so it can push updates to your Live Activity and notifications. For each tracked journey the backend stores:

  • The rail service identifier of the train you are tracking.
  • The station codes where you board and where you alight.
  • An Apple push token issued by Apple for your device's Live Activity.

That is the whole record. It contains no name, no email address, no account, and no location. The push token is issued by Apple, is specific to that Live Activity, and is used only to deliver ChooTrack's own journey updates.

This record is deleted when you stop tracking the journey, or when the journey completes. Nothing is retained afterwards.

6. Where the train data comes from

To show you a departure board or track a service, ChooTrack requests live rail data through its own backend from:

  • National Rail Enquiries, via the Rail Data Marketplace, for live departure and arrival information.
  • Realtime Trains, for service progress and calling-point detail.

These requests carry station codes and service identifiers. They do not carry your identity, and they are not used to build a profile of you.

7. What we never do

  • We do not sell your data. There is nothing to sell.
  • We do not share your data with advertisers, brokers, or partners.
  • We do not track you across other companies' apps or websites.
  • We do not embed analytics, advertising, attribution, or crash-reporting SDKs.
  • We do not read your contacts, photos, calendar, microphone, or camera. The app does not ask for them.

8. Legal basis and your rights (UK GDPR)

The only data processed that could conceivably identify a device is a tracked journey's push token, processed on the basis of your consent, given by choosing to track that journey and enabling notifications. You can withdraw it at any moment by untracking the journey, turning off notifications for ChooTrack in iOS Settings, or deleting the app.

Because there is no account, we hold nothing that can be looked up by name or email, so there is generally nothing to export or correct. If you believe we hold data about you and want it erased, contact us and we will do so. You also have the right to complain to the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

9. Children

ChooTrack is a general-audience rail information app. It is not directed at children, it does not knowingly collect data from children, and it contains no age-restricted content, no user-generated content, no chat, and no purchases.

10. Changes to this policy

If the app's data handling changes, this page will be updated and the “last updated” date at the top will change with it. Material changes will also be noted in the app's release notes.

11. Contact

Questions about privacy, or a request to delete something? Use the contact form on the support page — it reaches us directly.

Please include your iOS version and the app version (Settings → About) if your question is about a specific screen.
