What ChooTrack does

ChooTrack is a UK rail departures and arrivals app with one unusual trick: a time scrubber that drags the departure board backwards, up to two hours, so the train you just boarded (and every other app has already forgotten) is back on the board and one tap from being tracked.

  • Live boards for any UK National Rail station, departures or arrivals.
  • Rewind the board into the past and pick a service that has already left.
  • Guess my train: one location fix shortlists the live services you are most likely aboard.
  • Track a service for a live ETA, platform, next stop and calling points, with a Lock Screen Live Activity and a home-screen widget.
  • Pins, recents and a home station so your usual journey is one tap away.

Common questions

Why is a train missing from the board?

ChooTrack shows exactly what the rail data feeds publish for that station, and nothing more. A service can be absent because it has been cancelled and withdrawn from the feed, because it does not call at that station at all (some trains pass through without stopping), or because the operator has not yet published it.

Boards also have a horizon: very distant departures may not appear until closer to the time. If you are looking at today and the train has already gone, drag the scrubber backwards; departed services reappear with their actual departure time and platform.

If a train is on the physical station board but not in ChooTrack, that is worth reporting: send us the station, the scheduled time and the destination and we will look at what the feed returned.

Why is a delay different from the station board?

The screens at the station and the data ChooTrack receives do not always update at the same moment. Estimates are revised continuously as a train moves, and different systems pick up those revisions at slightly different times, so a minute or two of disagreement is normal.

ChooTrack blends two sources: National Rail Enquiries for the departure and arrival board, and Realtime Trains for where the service actually is along its route. When a service is disrupted, those two can briefly disagree with each other as well.

For anything safety-critical or time-critical, such as a last connection of the night, please treat the station's own displays and staff as the authority.

What is “Guess my train”?

You are already aboard and moving, and you never opened the app at the station. Tap Guess my train and ChooTrack takes a single location fix, matches your position against live services running nearby, and gives you a shortlist ranked from very likely to possible, with where each one is right now.

Pick one and you are straight into the normal tracking flow. It works best on the move, and it is a guess: on a corridor where several services run close together, check the destination before you track.

It needs location permission, asked once, When-In-Use only. The coordinate is used to identify the train and is not stored. See the privacy policy for the detail.

Why does a Live Activity need a real device?

Live Activities on the Lock Screen and Dynamic Island are kept up to date by Apple's push notification service. ChooTrack's backend watches your tracked service and pushes each update to the token Apple issued for that activity.

The iOS Simulator cannot receive those pushes, so a Live Activity in the Simulator will appear but never move. On a real iPhone it updates as the train does. Keep notifications enabled for ChooTrack, and keep Live Activities turned on in iOS Settings → ChooTrack.

If an activity goes stale on a real device, check that the phone has a data connection, that Low Power Mode or Focus is not suppressing updates, and that the journey has not already completed, which ends the activity by design.

How do I set a home station?

Open Settings in the app and choose Home station, then search for the station by name or CRS code. Fuzzy search accepts “pad”, “paddington” or “PAD”.

Your home station is what the app opens on and what the home-screen widget shows. You can change or clear it at any time; it is stored only on your device. Pinned stations and recents sit alongside it on the search screen for everything else.

Does ChooTrack cost anything, or need an account?

No and no. ChooTrack is free, has no in-app purchases, no subscription, and no sign-up. There is nothing to log into and nothing to restore.

How do I stop tracking a journey?

Open the Track tab and remove the journey. Tracking also ends on its own when the journey completes. Either way, the record the backend held for it (service id, boarding and alighting stations, push token) is deleted.

Known limitations

  • The data is only as good as the feeds. ChooTrack does not observe trains itself. Everything comes from National Rail Enquiries and Realtime Trains, and can lag, be incomplete, or be revised.
  • Disruption is where feeds struggle most. During major disruption, cancellations, diversions and platform changes can appear late or inconsistently.
  • Rewind is for today. The scrubber goes back up to two hours on the current day. For other dates, use the calendar to load that day's board instead.
  • Guess my train is a ranked guess, not a certainty, and it needs a live location fix and mobile signal.
  • UK National Rail only. No Underground, tram, metro, bus or non-UK services.
  • Live Activities and widgets need a real device with notifications enabled and a data connection.
  • Not a source of truth for boarding decisions. Always defer to station displays and staff.

Before you get in touch

Two minutes of detail saves a lot of back-and-forth. Please include:

  • Your iOS version and the ChooTrack version (Settings → About).
  • The station, date and scheduled time of the service involved.
  • What you expected, and what you actually saw.
  • A screenshot, if the problem is visible on screen.

We do not collect diagnostics automatically, so what you tell us is genuinely all we have to go on.

Contact support

Send a message and a human will read it — no account, no ticket number, no queue. Add your email only if you'd like a reply. Bug reports and “this train was wrong” reports are both welcome; the second kind is how the data problems get found.
